# Phase 2 ten-strategy parity suite

This local-only contract freezes the five committed trend-pullback designs and five committed close-confirmed breakout designs as `nora.phase2_ten_strategy_suite_v1`. It has two deliberately separate native roles:

1. **Embedded smoke canary:** exact structural reconciliation of the synthetic embedded OHLC fixture. This validates MQL5 generation, compilation, EX5 loading, tester startup, EA lifecycle, and deterministic fixture execution. Exact ledger equality is appropriate only inside this immutable fixture contract.
2. **Broker-native edge-survival validation:** similarity-based comparison of native MT5 results against Python/Rust research references using broker-native data and an explicitly pre-frozen budget map. It does not require trade-for-trade equality and does not reuse the embedded exact-reconciliation verdict.

Neither role is broad grammar admission, searchability, strategy search, or proof that Phase 2 is complete.

Trend-pullback uses only accepted EMA, Slope, ATR, Distance/ATR, and Cross semantics. Close-confirmed breakout uses only accepted Highest, Lowest, and Cross semantics, with the level window ending one completed bar before the decision bar. All decisions use completed bars, entries occur at the next open, positions cannot overlap, terminal source signals are not executed, and accepted execution precedence remains `gap → signal → time → intrabar` with pessimistic dual-touch handling.

The committed fixtures are synthetic embedded OHLC vectors. The embedded smoke canary does not use external market data, terminal history, timestamp conversion, account state, or trading operations as strategy inputs. MT5 may still perform ordinary symbol/tester setup; synchronization or cache-access wording is environmental evidence to classify, not by itself a failure.
